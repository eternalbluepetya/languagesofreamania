Progressive aspect

The ‘progressive’ aspect refers to actions that are happening at the time of speaking, such as I am learning.

Vayla uses an affix for progressive:

Progressive Prefix a-
ati /ˈati/
is learning


Habitual aspect

The ‘habitual’ aspect refers to actions that happen habitually, such as I learn (something new every day), as opposed to actions that happen once (I learned something).

Vayla uses a standalone particle word for habitual:

Habitual Particle before the verb: me -
me ti /me ti/
learns



Perfect aspect

The perfect aspect in English is exemplified in ‘I have read this book’, which expresses an event that took place before the time spoken but which has an effect on or is in some way still relevant to the present.

Vayla uses the word for ‘already’ sanser for the perfect aspect.
