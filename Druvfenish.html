Nouns

Nouns have six cases:

  • Ergative is the doer of a verb, when the verb is done to something: dog bites man.
  • Absolutive is used in two scenarios: the doer of a verb when not done to something (dog bites), and the done-to of a verb (man bites dog).
  • Genitive is the possessor of something: dog’s tail hits man.
  • Dative is the recipient of something: man gives ball to dog.
  • Locative is the location of something: man is in town.
  • Ablative is movement away from something: man walks from town.

Progressive aspect

The ‘progressive’ aspect refers to actions that are happening at the time of speaking, such as I am learning.

Druvfenish uses a standalone particle word for progressive:

Progressive Particle before the verb: tveft -
tveft srodkhi /tveft srodˈxi/
is learning


Habitual aspect

The ‘habitual’ aspect refers to actions that happen habitually, such as I learn (something new every day), as opposed to actions that happen once (I learned something).

Druvfenish uses a standalone particle word for habitual:

Habitual Particle before the verb: kvejs -
kveys srodkhi /kvejs srodˈxi/
learns
